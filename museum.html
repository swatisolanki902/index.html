Overview

The Pergamon Museum is one of the most renowned museums in Germany and the world. Located on Berlin’s Museum Island, it showcases ancient architecture, artifacts, and cultural treasures from around the globe.

It’s part of the Staatliche Museen zu Berlin (State Museums of Berlin).

History

Built between 1910 and 1930, the Pergamon Museum was designed by Alfred Messel and Ludwig Hoffmann. It was created to house the incredible archaeological finds of the 19th and 20th centuries, including the world-famous Pergamon Altar.
